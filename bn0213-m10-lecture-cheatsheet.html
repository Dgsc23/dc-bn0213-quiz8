Why lipids behave the way they do

Definition
A lipid is defined by being insoluble in water (hydrophobic). Long nonpolar C-C and C-H chains give water nothing to grab.
Soluble in
Organic solvents: ether, chloroform, benzene, acetone. (You clean spilled paint with acetone. Like dissolves like.)
Energy
Those same C-C and C-H bonds are high-energy, which is why fat carries more than double the calories per gram of sugar.

The jobs of lipids

Energy
Stored as triacylglycerol (= triglyceride).
Cell membranes
Phospholipids.
Vitamin absorption
The fat-soluble vitamins D, E, A, K (his slide writes them DEAK). No dietary fat = you do not absorb them. A fat-free salad costs you these; add walnuts or olive oil.
Steroid hormones
Made from cholesterol (testosterone, estrogen).
Protection
Adipose (triacylglycerols) pads the internal organs.
Electrical insulation
Myelin sheaths around axons.
Lung surfactant
Dipalmitoyl lecithin.

Triacylglycerol and wax

He put the drawn triacylglycerol up at 101:24 and joked: "here's your tag, you're going to have to draw this out on the exam. Just kidding."
Triacylglycerol (Objective 1)
Glycerol + 3 fatty acids, joined by 3 ester bonds. (Acid + alcohol, lose a water = ester.) Stored in adipocytes in animals; the same molecule is the oil in plant seeds.
Wax
A fatty acid esterified to a monohydric long-chain alcohol. Example: cetyl palmitate, the major component of spermaceti wax.
Wax function
Waterproofing and energy storage. A cactus has a waxy coat; your skin has an oily one. Lose that barrier (burns) and you dehydrate.

The fatty acid itself

"Watch this, everybody, and hopefully you will remember this forever. We say it's saturated because it's saturated with hydrogens." (53:03)
Structure
A long-chain carboxylic acid. Chain length C4 to C24. General formula R-COOH.
The two ends
The carboxyl end (COOH, the acid end) and the methyl end, also called the omega end. "Make sure you know that there's those two terminus."
Saturated
Saturated with hydrogens. No carbon-to-carbon double bonds; every carbon holds all the hydrogen it can.
Unsaturated
Has one or more C=C double bonds, so it is missing hydrogens there. MUFA = one double bond. PUFA = more than one.
The bend
A double bond is rigid and kinks the chain. Single bonds swivel, so saturated chains hang straight.

THE FOUR S's — his own red pen

"Here's an easy way I want you to remember this. Everyone, please pay attention. Make notes here." (68:59)
Saturated
Sat · Single (bonds) · Straight (no bend) · Solid at room temp.
Unsaturated
Un · Double (bonds) · Bend · Liquid.
Why
Straight chains stack tightly (like uncooked spaghetti), so they are solid. Bent chains cannot pack, so they stay liquid.
His examples
The white fat on a filet mignon is solid, and melts on the barbecue. Cheese is solid on the counter. Olive oil pours.
Rule of thumb
Animal products are mostly saturated; plants are mostly unsaturated.

Nomenclature (Objective 3)

"Make sure you understand that nomenclature. You will see that on a test." (65:30)
Suffix
-anoic = saturated. Eicosanoic acid (= arachidic acid), found in peanut oil. -enoic = unsaturated. Eicosapentaenoic acid (EPA), an omega-3 in cold-water fish.
Alpha / beta
Numbering from the carboxyl end: the carbon next to COOH is alpha, the next is beta, then down the Greek alphabet.
Omega
The last carbon (methyl end), because omega is the last Greek letter.
Omega family
Named for the position of the FIRST double bond counted from the methyl end. 3 carbons in = omega-3; 6 in = omega-6; 9 in = omega-9.
Shorthand
C18:3 = 18 carbons long, 3 double bonds. Carbons before the colon, double bonds after.
His figure
Omega-3: alpha-linolenic (ALA) C18:3 · EPA C20:5 · DHA C22:6. Omega-6: linoleic (LA) C18:2 · arachidonic (AA) C20:4.

Essential fatty acids

Definition
Your body cannot synthesize them, so they must be eaten. "We have to eat them because we cannot make them."
The two parents
Linoleic acid (omega-6) and alpha-linolenic acid (omega-3). Both are PUFAs. Omega-9 is NOT essential.
The branches
Omega-6 leads down to arachidonic acid. Omega-3 leads to EPA, then DHA.
Eicosanoids (Objective 5)
Signalling molecules built from 20-carbon PUFAs (eicosa = twenty). That is why arachidonic acid, C20:4, matters.
Omega-3 clinical significance
Keeps membranes fluid; displaces arachidonic acid from the phospholipids of platelets, endothelial and inflammatory cells; lowers serum triglycerides; cardioprotective. Sources: cold-water fish (salmon, tuna, herring), walnuts, flaxseed.
Western diet
Takes in far too much omega-6.

Cis vs trans (Objective 4)

Cis
Both hydrogens on the SAME side of the C=C. Causes a bend. Found in natural food: almonds, avocados, bacon, canola oil.
Trans
Hydrogens on OPPOSITE sides. No bend, stays straight, so it packs and behaves like a saturated fat even though it is unsaturated.
How trans fats are made
PARTIAL hydrogenation. (Complete hydrogenation would give a saturated fat instead.) This is his Study Check answer.
Health
Trans fats raise cardiovascular disease and diabetes risk. He noted the mechanism is unclear.
Where they are
Naturally in small amounts: milk, eggs, beef. From hydrogenation: deep-fried foods, baked goods and cookies, crackers and chips, margarine, vegetable shortening.

Phospholipids and sphingolipids (Objective 6)

"This is one you should know for sure" (106:19, lecithin) · "You should definitely know this" (108:25, sphingomyelin)
The recipe
Alcohol + fatty acid + phosphate + amino alcohol. The core alcohol names the subtype.
Glycerophospholipid
Alcohol = glycerol. Example: phosphatidylcholine = lecithin.
Sphingophospholipid
Alcohol = sphingosine. Example: sphingomyelin.
Lecithin
Dipalmitoyl lecithin IS the lung surfactant. It lowers the surface tension of the alveoli, which prevents alveolar collapse. A premature infant has not made it yet: respiratory distress syndrome.
Sphingomyelin
A large portion of the myelin sheath (nerves, brain, lungs, liver). Myelin insulates axons.
Glycolipids
Ceramide + carbohydrate (glyco = sugar).
Cerebroside vs ganglioside
Cerebroside = ONE sugar (glucose or galactose), located in myelin, brain and nerves. Ganglioside = an oligosaccharide (many sugars), located in the gray matter.

Lysosomal storage diseases

The principle
The lysosomal enzyme is missing, so its substrate accumulates.
Tay-Sachs
Missing hexosaminidase A → GM2 ganglioside accumulates. Cherry-red spot in the macula, blindness, psychomotor retardation, exaggerated startle reflex, death usually before age 2. No hepatosplenomegaly.
Gaucher
Missing glucocerebrosidase → glucocerebroside accumulates. Hepatosplenomegaly, erosion of bone and fractures, pancytopenia/thrombocytopenia.
Niemann-Pick
Missing sphingomyelinase → sphingomyelin accumulates. Cherry-red spot AND hepatosplenomegaly, mental retardation, early death.
His mnemonic
Cherry Trees Never Grow Tall In Sand = CRAO (central retinal artery occlusion), Trauma, Niemann-Pick, Gaucher, Tay-Sachs, Sandhoff. (The causes of a cherry-red spot.)
His exam tip
Cherry-red spot + CNS symptoms but NO hepatosplenomegaly = Tay-Sachs (Niemann-Pick would give the big liver and spleen). He ranked board likelihood: 1) Tay-Sachs 2) Niemann-Pick 3) Gaucher.
